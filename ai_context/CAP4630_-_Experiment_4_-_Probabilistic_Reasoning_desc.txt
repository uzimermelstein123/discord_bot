<p><strong>The Monty Hall Problem</strong></p>
<ul style="list-style-type: disc;">
<li>There are 3 doors: behind one is a car, behind two are goats.</li>
<li>You choose one door.</li>
<li>The host knows where the car is.</li>
<li>The host opens a different door, always revealing a goat.</li>
<li>Now only two doors remain:</li>
<li>your original choice and</li>
<li>one other.</li>
<li>You must decide: Stay or Switch?</li>
</ul>
<p>Extra:</p>
<p>What would be your decision if we had 4 doors, 5 doors, 10 doors?&nbsp;</p>
<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><strong>Task:</strong></p>
<p><span>Model the Monty Hall Problem using a Bayesian Network.</span></p>
<ul>
<li aria-level="1"><span>Define the random variables (CarDoor, PlayerChoice, HostOpens).</span></li>
<li aria-level="1"><span>Specify the dependency structure between them.</span></li>
<li aria-level="1"><span>Encode the host’s conditional behavior.</span></li>
<li aria-level="1"><span>Perform probabilistic inference.</span></li>
<li aria-level="1"><span>Verify whether switching produces a better winning probability or not..</span></li>
</ul>
