<p>4. &nbsp;Try your hand at writing a four line &nbsp;"I'm From" poem. If you aren't sure what an "I'm From" poem is, here are some instructions from the teaching website "Now Spark Creativity:"&nbsp;</p>
<p>"Brainstorm about sensory experiences, sayings, and people from your childhoods and then choose your favorite bits and pieces to put into the basic structure of an “I am from poem,” each verse of which goes something like this:</p>
<p>“I am from….<br>From … and …<br>I am from … and …..</p>
<p><strong>So if a student had brainstormed imagery like this:&nbsp;</strong><strong><br></strong>raisins lined up on crunchy peanut butter celery</p>
<p>my worn-out red ping pong paddle with the handle duct-taped on</p>
<p>my Dad’s loud laugh</p>
<p>my brother’s protection</p>
<div><strong>Then a stanza might look like:</strong></div>
<div>I am from the crunch of ants on a log for snack after school.</div>
<div>From Dad’s loud laugh and Taj’s brotherly protection.</div>
<div>I am from a worn out ping pong paddle with a winning record."</div>
